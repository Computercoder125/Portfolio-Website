I am Sean Gor, a current sophomore at Saint Joseph's University majoring in computer science and minoring in Data Science.



I have always been driven by understanding how things work underneath the hood. Ever since I was a little kid, I was fascinated about how a phone could perform various complex tasks with one touch of a finger, or how a keyboard on a computer worked to display a key on an internet screen. I would always look up how to utilize different functions of devices that others may have overlooked. This lead me to enroll in coding camps, where I strengthened my logical and programming skills in object-oriented programming languages like Python, C++ and C#, web based languages like HTML and CSS, and GUI (guided user interfaces) like Scenebuilder in Java. In fact, this website was designed through Node.js, html and css! I am looking forward to any opportunities provided to me in the fields of software and cybersecurity development, especially in the rapidly advancing field of AI.


At Saint Joseph's, I am a co-president of the Computer Science Club, a community where programmers of all experiences can fiddle with different frameworks / programming languages, and work together to create mind-blowing yet fun projects. I also am currently a peer tutor and teaching assistant for lower level computer science classes, helping other students master the material and become better programmers. Additionally, I receive piano lessons and am on the jazz band where I play challenging yet enjoyable classical and jazz pieces.

While not in classes or coding, I enjoy working out, playing classical and jazz songs on the piano, and hanging out with friends and family. I believe self-care is just as important as a strong work ethic.

Feel free to reach out to me with any questions/concerns, and I look forward to connecting with you.
